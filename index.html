About

I am a Post-Doctoral Research Associate at the University of Maryland, working in precision and digital agriculture. My research focuses on UAV-based imaging, machine learning and deep learning for plant phenotyping, soil moisture prediction, evapotranspiration estimation, and sustainable agricultural water management.

Our Mission

The AgroSensing & AI Lab focuses on AI, remote sensing, and precision agriculture for sustainability. We combine UAV and satellite data with machine learning to support water management, crop phenotyping, soil health, and environmental resilience—turning data into actionable intelligence for farmers and land managers.

Main Research Areas

My expertise spans AI and remote sensing for crop and livestock systems. In addition to precision agriculture and water management, I have experience in AI for animal disease detection and management. In work focused on cattle in Ethiopia, I contributed to an expert system integrated with deep learning image processing for timely livestock disease diagnosis: symptom images (cell-phone captured) were classified by a CNN with 95% accuracy, and a reasoner combined image results with location and text inputs to deliver rapid, effective diagnosis—addressing a critical gap in settings where livestock are essential for food security and livelihoods.
